Sustainability

To analyze the success of this project, its outcomes are analysed from the three main perspectives of sustainability:

  • Environmental
  • Economic
  • Social

Environmental Sustainability

Pros

  • Renewable energy source
  • Mostly comprised of wood and reusable or recyclable parts
  • No emissions

Cons

  • Production of solar panels can produce environmentally harmful waste
  • Can be expensive
  • Similar to some batteries, can be envionmentally harmful if disposed of improperly

The current model relies on a single solar panel and will optimistically take about 16 years to pay itself off; however, if more panels are added, this time can be lessened.

In the figure above is the expected trend for the time expected for a unit to pay itself off vs. the number of solar panels that can be used. Social Sustainability

Public view of Solar Panels

As far as the project in the eyes of the public, we are optimistic as to how we expect people to think of our project. Solar panels have a healthy reputation in society as a green source of energy.

In Regards to Transportation

With personal electric vehicles becoming more popular by the year, more people are beginning to invest in these modes of transportation. This end product of this project will be something that people can put in their home or it can be something that is rented out to various institutions so that people have somewhere to park there vehicle to charge. It is also hopeful that this will encourage more people to invest in green transportation.
