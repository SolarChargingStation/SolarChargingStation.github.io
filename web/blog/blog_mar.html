      • The 3D printed gears were a success as far as we can tell. The teeth are meshing well and they feel rigid enough for use.
        • The primary gear wasn't quite wide enough for the motor shaft but a little bit of sanding remedied this.

    • Mar. 17, 2020

        • We will continue working on the project despite all of this.
      • We received the DC motor and Linear Actuator in the mail a few days ago
        • The DC motor and its built-in encoder work well.
        • The linear actuator was extending and retracting but the PWM position feedback wasn't working as expected.
          • Tried to fix it and now it doesn't work at all.
          • We will likely resort to using the linear actuator that was used for testing.

    • Mar. 21, 2020

      • Obtained permission from instructor to use lab's linear actuator in project.

    • Mar. 25, 2020

      • Due to COVID-19, project may not be fully assembled but several of the key parts of the project will be in working order.
      • Tested encoder and H-bridge with DC motor
        • There are 11 encoder ticks with every turn of motor
        • The motor itself has a turn ratio of 1:522, meaning that there are actually 522*11 encoder ticks per revolution of the shaft... 5742 ticks per revolution.
        • Furthermore, this means that with a 1:5 gear ratio, there will be 28710 encoder ticks per 360 degrees of the platform's revolution
